# GDI Gate Taxonomy

GDI recognizes five decision outcomes and four gate levels. Together they form the governance classification layer that determines what level of human deliberation a decision requires.

The taxonomy is informed by phase-gate decision methodology from regulated industries, where decisions are discrete, documented events with defined governance consequences.

---

## The Five Decision Outcomes

| Outcome | Definition | Governance Implication |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| Go | Decision proceeds with full confidence and complete evidence | Standard GDR logged. Downstream propagation tracked. |
| Conditional Go | Decision proceeds with explicit conditions, owned follow-ups, and verification checkpoints | Conditions schema-validated. Each condition has named owner and deadline. |
| Defer | Decision paused due to insufficient evidence or unresolved dependencies | Re-entry criteria defined. Evidence gaps documented. Timeline set. |
| No-Go | Decision explicitly rejected with documented rationale | Re-entry logic captured. Stopping treated as risk reduction. |
| Escalate | Decision exceeds current authority scope or policy boundaries | Escalation path, receiving authority, and outcome all logged. |

**Critical design principle:** Defer and No-Go are first-class outcomes with the same governance rigor as Go decisions. The decision to stop or pause is a governed artifact, not an absence of decision.

Aviation's Crew Resource Management philosophy informs this design: the decision to go around on a landing approach receives the same procedural treatment as the decision to land. Stopping early is risk reduction, and risk reduction deserves a governed record.

---

## The Four Gate Levels

Gate levels answer a different question from decision outcomes. Where outcomes describe *what was decided*, gate levels describe *what level of human deliberation this decision requires*.

| Gate | Name | Human Deliberation Required | Trigger Conditions |
|------|------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| Gate 1 | Routine Execution | None at decision time. Automated documentation. | Policy allowed. Confidence in GREEN zone. Routine tool class. |
| Gate 2 | Documented Delegation | None at decision time. Upstream delegation invoked and recorded. | Policy allowed. Delegation reference present. Decision within pre-authorized scope. |
| Gate 3 | Elevated Review | Mandatory. A designated reviewer must acknowledge the artifact before or immediately after execution. | Policy allowed but: confidence in AMBER zone, elevated tool class, novel conditions, or approaches policy boundary. |
| Gate 4 | Hard Escalation | Execution halts. Human authority required before any action proceeds. | Policy denied. Confidence in RED zone. Out-of-bounds tool class. Conditions the system cannot classify. |

---

## Gate Classification Is Normative, Not Observational

Gate classification must be defined by the institution and applied by the governance layer. It cannot be generated by the model being governed. Self-certification is not governance.

The four-gate taxonomy is an institutional normative claim. The organization defines what "elevated review" means for their domain, what tools trigger Gate 4, and what confidence thresholds map to what gates. GDI provides the architecture. Institutions provide the calibration.

---

## Relationship to Confidence Zones

Confidence zone assessment is one input to gate classification. Gate classification may also be driven by:

- Tool class (some tools always trigger Gate 3 or Gate 4 regardless of confidence)
- Policy decision (a policy denial always produces Gate 4)
- Institutional rules (custom overrides for specific agent types or contexts)

## GDR Field

Gate classification is captured in the `gate_classification` object of the GDR:

```json
{
  "gate_classification": {
    "gate": "gate_3_elevated_review",
    "gate_rationale": "Tool class database_write requires elevated review. Confidence 0.85 above threshold but tool class policy overrides.",
    "escalation_required": false,
    "escalation_trigger": null,
    "delegation_reference": null
  }
}
```
