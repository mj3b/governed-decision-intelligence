# About the Author

[![ORCID](https://img.shields.io/badge/ORCID-0009--0001--8121--2878-brightgreen)](https://orcid.org/0009-0001-8121-2878)

**Mark Julius Banasihan** designs decision governance systems for high-stakes environments where speed, accountability, and defensibility must coexist.

His work addresses a persistent failure mode in complex organizations: AI-informed decisions that are technically sound yet governance-fragile because their rationale, evidence base, and accountability chain cannot be reconstructed under regulatory scrutiny. This failure becomes more pronounced as AI accelerates analysis and execution without comparable discipline in decision ownership and documentation.

GDI emerged from this perspective. It is the domain-agnostic specification that RGDS — his biopharma-focused reference implementation — generalized into. Both are presented as open research artifacts to be tested, challenged, and adapted. Their value is measured by whether they help organizations govern consequential AI decisions with the rigor that high-stakes environments require.

---

### Research and Practice Orientation

Mark's approach draws from decision science, AI safety research, organizational psychology, and regulated industry governance patterns. His work emphasizes:

- Decision ownership as the primary governance object
- Evidence classification and completeness as governance inputs, not afterthoughts
- Accountability chains that satisfy the tracing condition for meaningful human control
- Governance architecture that remains valid if AI outputs are removed

The objective is governance that operates continuously and automatically — infrastructure, not oversight.

---

### Core Areas of Focus

**Decision Architecture and Runtime AI Governance**

Designing decision-first specifications for consequential AI-informed workflows where human accountability must be singular, explicit, and audit-ready.

**AI Safety Policy Interface**

The connection between pre-deployment capability evaluations and runtime decision governance — the eval-to-governance handoff — is the research frontier this work aims to advance.

**Applied Research Translation**

Converting published research in decision science, AI safety, and regulatory compliance into bounded, falsifiable design principles with clear failure modes.

**Open Source Prior Art Strategy**

Apache 2.0 licensing and Git commit timestamps as the mechanism for establishing prior art without competing on proprietary IP.

### Working Philosophy

People define correctness. Systems exist to preserve it.

Governance maturity is inseparable from execution quality. Credibility is built through structure, not assertion.

The decision to stop, defer, or escalate deserves the same governance rigor as the decision to proceed.
