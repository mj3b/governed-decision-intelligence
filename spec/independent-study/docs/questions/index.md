# Research Questions

This study examines GDI across ten questions. Each question is analyzed with supporting evidence from the 26-source bibliography, quantified where the evidence supports it, and scoped explicitly where it does not. Contribution claims are timestamped to May 2026.

---

## Structure of Each Question

Each research question page follows a consistent structure:

1. **Answer in brief** — the core finding in one paragraph
2. **Evidence base** — quantified claims with inline citations
3. **Mechanism** — how the proposed intervention produces the stated outcome
4. **Limitations** — what the analysis does not address
5. **Implementation guidance** — what a practitioner or researcher needs to act on the finding

---

## The Ten Questions

**Foundational (Q1–Q4):** Establish whether the gap is real, whether GDI closes it, and where the architecture's hard limits lie.

- [Q1: The Decision-Layer Gap](q1.md) — Does a structural gap exist between what governance frameworks require at the decision level and what any published open specification provides?
- [Q2: The Eval-to-Governance Handoff](q2.md) — What does the handoff from pre-deployment capability evaluation to runtime decision governance require, and does GDI satisfy it?
- [Q3: Confidence Score Trustworthiness](q3.md) — Under what conditions is model-reported confidence a valid governance input for GDI's threshold model?
- [Q4: The Comprehension Threshold Problem](q4.md) — Under what conditions is named human authorization itself insufficient for meaningful accountability?

**Original Contributions (Q5–Q6):** Problems absent from the published literature. GDI either names them formally for the first time or exposes them as open questions the field must address.

- [Q5: Evidence Expiration as Governance Parameter](q5.md) — What is the mechanism by which expired evidence should propagate through a governed decision record?
- [Q6: Conduct Collapse Detection](q6.md) — Can GDI's architecture produce observable signals that precede the progressive degradation of governance behavior?

**Operational (Q7–Q9):** Positioning GDI relative to adjacent work, implementation constraints, and framework compatibility evidence.

- [Q7: Delegation Provenance Completeness](q7.md) — What does GDI require that cryptographic delegation protocols do not provide?
- [Q8: CI/CD Governance at Agentic Speed](q8.md) — What governance latency does schema validation introduce, and when does asynchronous validation become necessary?
- [Q9: Framework Compatibility as Implementation Evidence](q9.md) — For six major frameworks, which requirement does GDI satisfy, through which mechanism, with what operational evidence?

**Regulatory (Q10):** The policy case.

- [Q10: Regulatory Trajectory](q10.md) — Which regulatory instruments create the first mandatory demand for GDI-class artifacts, and on what timeline?
